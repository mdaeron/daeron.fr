
# Cookbook

Protocols and recipes

## 1 - Heated gas preparation

### Protocol

- prepare, evacuate and degas quartz tube (OD = 6 mm; L = 15 cm)
- carefully remove water from a sample-sized aliquot of CO2 by repeated cryogenic separation using dry ice/ethanol slush and liquid N<sub>2</sub>
- transfer CO2 to quartz tube and flame-seal the tube
- heat tube at 1000 °C for 2 h
- remove tube from furnace and quench it in liquid N<sub>2</sub>
- transfer CO2 to prep line

### Notes
- heated CO2 should remain stochastic for days or weeks, but we always analyze it within 30–60 mn of quenching

## 2 - Equilibrated gas preparation

### Preparation Protocol

- prepare pyrex tube (OD = 6 mm; L = 20 cm)
- inject 60 μL H2O into the tube
- freeze the water in the tube using dry ice/ethanol slush
- evacuate the tube and transfer sample-sized CO2 into it
- flame-seal the tube
- transfer the tube to water-bath (25 °C in our case)
- leave enough time for full isotopic equilibration

### Recollection Protocol

- collect tube from the water bath and submerge the lower tip (containing the water) in liquid N<sub>2</sub>
- transfer the tube and dewar to prep line
- connect the upper tip of the tube to prep line and evacuate dead space (10-20 mn in our case)
- replace liquid N<sub>2</sub> with dry ice/ethanol slush to release CO2
- break the tube to inject CO2 into the prep line

### Notes

- we only use seawater or meteoric water, and check its Δ<sup>17</sup>O value
- avoid evaporatively enriched water unless you can check its Δ<sup>17</sup>O value
- in our case, minimal isotopic equilibration time is 48 h, but in practice we prepare equilibrated CO2 weeks in advance

## 3 - Evaporative enrichment of water

- start with a 5 L glass vial filled with 3 L water
- Ideally, the water-atmosphere interface should have a large surface (water level should start below the point where the vial diameter starts decreasing)
- Using a heating magnetic stirrer, keep water at a constant value between 70 and 90 deg C, and stir the water continuousy
- Monitor the water level closely for a few days
- Collect the water after 85-95 % evaporation
- In repeated experiments at LSCE, this protocol consistently yielded Δ^17^O anomalies well within 40 ppm of starting values.
